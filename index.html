About Me

CV

I am a PhD candidate in Mathematics at ICMAT-CSIC with a background that bridges mathematics, physics, statistics, and machine learning. I completed double bachelor's degrees in Mathematics and Physics at the University of Valladolid and later earned a Master's in Statistics for Data Science at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, where I strengthened my statistical and computational foundation.

My trajectory has moved from rigorous theoretical training toward research problems where modelling, inference, and decision-making meet real applications. I am especially interested in probability, Bayesian methods, ranking and preference models, artificial intelligence, and analytically grounded ways of turning data into useful decisions.

5SYSORM Book of Abstracts - 2025

A two-level Plackett-Luce model for preference modelling in route choice

Abstract. This contribution studies a Bayesian preference model for route choice based on a two-level Plackett-Luce formulation. It combines synthetic data generation, MCMC inference with NUTS, and predictive analysis to learn preference structures and support intelligent decision-making in mobility settings.

Teaching

Teaching

My teaching experience has focused on undergraduate mathematics, especially in courses where conceptual clarity and careful problem solving are central.

Discrete Mathematics

Undergraduate course

This teaching involved supporting students through the foundations of discrete mathematical reasoning, including logic, proof techniques, combinatorics, recurrence relations, and graph-based thinking. The emphasis was on helping students gain confidence with formal arguments while connecting abstract tools to structured problem solving.

Mathematical Analysis

Undergraduate course

This teaching focused on core ideas from mathematical analysis, including limits, continuity, differentiation, integration, and rigorous argumentation around sequences and functions. Special attention was given to building intuition together with precision, so students could move from computational techniques to genuine mathematical understanding.
